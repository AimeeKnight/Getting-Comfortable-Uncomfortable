Sometimes you learn best from people one step ahead of you.

  • Research shows that often times you think you know less than you do. Working with others can help you normalize your thoughts.
  • Often times for beginners it’s hard to keep everything on our heads. Pairing up can help as it allows you to step back for periods of time to rest and absorb.

Seeing the code base for the first time...

Work your butt off…
but be smart about it.

  • Plan first. (Yep... failing to plan is planning to fail.)
  • Style guides, testing, research new libraries or standards.
  • Realize it’s a mindset... a thought process. Not something you can force.
  • Always reflect on what you've learned to make it stick. (Learning through reflection.)
  • Refactor
  • Practice makes permanent.
    • Being a developer isn’t about writing code, it’s about producing working software while simultaneously hitting business goals and maintaining expectations. Don’t ever check in code that isn’t a little better off than before you started. Maintainable code is code that is easily readable, extendable, and testable by outside developers.

Ask us questions... a lot!

  • Think of us as your past self
  • A lot of the time we don’t have the same context to rely on as you do.
  • You might need to explain things differently to us that you would a senior developer.
  • If you and I were watching the Olympics together and I told you 'she cheated her double axel and had a bad wrap’, you’d have no idea what I meant. What I could say to you though is; 'she… actually rotated her foot before taking off into the air (which is called cheating), and her left leg came up above her knee in the air (that’s called wrapping) which is really poor style’.
  • You might be fuzzy on what a double axel is, so you go Google that, then you follow up by trying to figure out what the heck ‘cheated’ and ‘wrap’ mean in this context, and before you know it what could be explained in one sentence, has turned into a 30 minute rabbit hole.
  • You might think this is a pain, but it will help you grow as you’ll have to think about a problem in a very different way.
